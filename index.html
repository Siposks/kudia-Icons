Nshima

Nshima is a staple food in Zambia – a very thick and smooth solid porridge made from ground maize. It is quite similar to the Kenyan ugali, Zimbabwean sadza and the north Italian dish Polenta.

Cooking Nshima

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups of maize flour
  • 2 1/2 cups of water

To make the Nshima start by adding some hot water to the maize and stir it in to make a paste. To thicken up the Nshima add more maize to the pot. Make sure you don’t have any lumps and the liquid is gone. Stir your Nshima until it is smooth.

Kapenta

The Tanganyika sardine is known as kapenta or matemba in Zambia, Malawi and Zimbabwe. Kapenta is two species, both of which are small, planktivorous, pelagic, freshwater clupeid originating from Lake Tanganyika in Zambia.
